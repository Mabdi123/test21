Define Business Unit Attributes

The primary purpose of a Hierarchy of Business Units is to ensure that alerts and reports are viewed and dealt with by the right users (those who require this information for their tasks) and only by the right users.

You can define Business Unit Attributes at any stage while creating hierarchies. However, since these attributes are global, they should be defined and already available when defining new business units. Consequently, the Attributes are usually defined first, before you begin to set up the hierarchies.

Attributes can be defined to specify any type of data that may pertain to a business unit. For example, you may define an attribute called Address which enables you to specify the address of any Business Unit that represents an actual branch office. When you define attributes you must specify the data type for the attribute (e.g., string).

To define the first Business Unit Attribute:

  1. From the Configuration menu, select the Business Units Attributes option.

To add a new attribute to the library of Business Unit Attributes:

  1. From the Business Units Attributes window, click to add a new row to the list of attributes.
  1. Provide a name and select the appropriate data type for the new attribute.
  1. Once you are satisfied with the attributes that you want to be available for the Business Units, click OK at the bottom of the Business Unit Attributes window to save these definitions.

The newly defined attributes appear within the Attributes tab for each business unit that is defined within your system. You can create new attributes at any time and then return to existing Business Units in order to assign a value for the newly created attribute, as shown below.

Once the Business Unit Attributes are defined, you can map Report parameters to these attributes when setting up Report definitions (see Report Parameters).

Business Calendar Lists

The Business Calendar platform lists enable you to configure global deadlines, email notifications and reminders for work item workflow steps according to calendars that have business days logic included. This means you can designate dates for certain calendars that are not business days and are not included in the deadline count.

The following platform lists are automatically installed.

  • Business Calendar – Days: Allows you to designate non-business days for a specific calendar. Includes the following fields.

  • calendar_name – The name of the calendar you are assigning the date to.
  • date – The date you want to designate as a non-business day.
  • description – A description of the calendar.
  • Business Calendar – BU Associations: Allows you to assign a calendar to a business unit, so that items in that BU are connected to a specific calendar. Includes the following fields.

  • bu_identifier – The BU you are associating to a calendar. Using the BU picker, you select from your list of BUs.

NOTE:

The Business Unit field displays the BU identifier in the RCM UI platform list grid.

  • calendar_name – The name of the calendar you are assigning the date to.

The following is an example of the Business Calendar – Days General tab.

Selecting one of the checkboxes enables you to:

Four Eye Review – Enforce an approval for every new item that is added to the relevant business calendar list by the RCM user. Only the approved items are counted in the Business Days calculation.

Support multi-tenant mode – Support multi-tenant mode for every new item that is added to the relevant business calendar list by the RCM user. This checkbox is selected by default for both calendar lists.

NOTE:

In a multi tenant environment, if one of the calendar lists (or both) support host values, then the calculation takes the host configuration into account in the list that supports the host values.

Support test values – Support tuning values for every new record that is added to the relevant business calendar list by the RCM user. Items in test status are not counted in the Business Days calculation.

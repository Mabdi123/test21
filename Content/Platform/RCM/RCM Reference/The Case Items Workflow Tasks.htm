The Case Items Workflow Tasks Table

A task is an action that user must do before moving to the next step. A step may have one or more tasks and some tasks include specific deadlines.

Depending on your organization’s implementation, deadlines are calculated according to a specific time frame which may include configuration based on business days calendars. These calendars enable you to set specific days as non business days which are not calculated in workflow and step deadlines. For details on platform list calendars, see Business Calendar Lists.

A step may include no tasks.

The tasks for the current step automatically appears in the tasks table. Each task includes a link that moves you directly to the window where the task should be carried out.

The tasks table includes the following columns:

Option

Description

A dot icon indicates that this task is mandatory. You cannot proceed to the next step if you did not complete the mandatory tasks in a step.

Task Name

Clicking the Task Name link moves you to the relevant page for the specific task.

Confirmed by

Indicates the name of the user that confirmed to do the task. This column is automatically filled in when the user clicks the Check button.

On Date

Indicates the date when the task was confirmed. This column is automatically filled in when the Check button is clicked.

Click this button once the task has finished. The Confirmed by and On Date columns are then automatically filled in.

Once you have finished your tasks, you have the option to move to one of the available steps.

To move to another step:

  1. From the Workflow toolbar, click the Next steps drop-down menu.

    2. A drop-down menu appears.

  1. Select one of the available steps in the drop-down menu.

  1. Once you are in the new step, perform its related tasks and move to the next selected steps.
  • You may also carry out action(s) in a particular step that are not in the list of tasks.
  • Certain steps may prevent you from performing specific actions (e.g., adding a note).
  • A step may include a deadline. After the deadline occurs, the specific step may automatically move to another step. In such scenarios, you are usually prompted with reminders that you have a limited amount of time to perform all the tasks in the step and move to the next step.
  • Certain steps may be disabled according to relevant review rules.

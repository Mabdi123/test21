About Permissions

The includes the following concepts, which are used during the process of assigning permissions for the actions and views:

Option

Description

Users

Users are individuals within an organization that have access to .

Groups

A logical group of users used to simplify role assignment.   Groups may contain users, other groups, or a combination of the two.

Roles

Roles reflect the roles that users perform within their organization, such as Supervisors, Managers, Administrators, Compliance Officers, etc. Each role definition contains a set of granular permissions to data and features of the application. For example, the Administrator’s role includes permission to add, edit and delete users and groups from the .

A role defines the permissions for the actions and views within each of the modules of the Risk Case Manager. You can set, within the same role, different permissions for different work item types or for different report types. For example, you can stipulate that a specific role (for example, Compliance Officer) includes permission to view all work items and to perform all actions on work items except the action change score, which can only be performed for Alert Type TRR.

Business Unit Hierarchies

Each organization is mapped according to at least one organizational hierarchy. This hierarchy usually reflects the divisions, departments etc. within the organization. There may be more than one hierarchy associated with the company. For example one may reflect the Business structure of the company (for example, CEO, VPs, Managers etc.) while another one reflects the regional organization of the company.

Business Units

Business units represent a line of business, department, or other logical organizational unit that work is performed within.  All workflow is on work items owned by single business unit.

The primary purpose of the business unit is to ensure that work items and reports are viewed and dealt with by the right users (those who require this information for their tasks) and only by the right users.

For example, those users who must see specific work items or reports associated with USA, in order to perform their roles, see the information that they require and do not receive irrelevant work items (for example, about Asia) that do not pertain to their tasks.

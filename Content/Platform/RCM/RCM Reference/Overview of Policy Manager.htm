Overview of Policy Manager

Policy Manager is used to further modify criteria for work item generation. For example, the user may adjust a rule in order to enable transactions for VIP customers or delay transactions from suspicious IP address ranges.

To use the Policy Manager:

  1. Click the Policy Manager tab.
  1. Select the relevant policy from the Policy Type drop-down menu.

  1. The following subtabs appear:
    • Draft Policy: enables you to create a Draft Policy for this Policy Type. Once the Draft Policy is complete it can be activated in order to become the Active Policy and appear in the Active Policy Tab. See The Draft Policy Tab.
    • Policy Activation: enables you to review a draft policy that is about to be activated, analyze the difference between this policy and the current policy, and confirm it as an active policy. Note that this tab’s name switches to Pending Policy when a new policy is activated. See Activate a Draft Policy - The Policy Activation Tab.
    • Active Policy: displays the policy that is currently enabled for this Policy Type. Initially, the Active Policy tab is blank. See The Active Policy Tab.
    • Historical Policies: displays a list of policies that were requested to be activated in the past and enables you to view a specific policy (includes both successful and failed policies). See Historical Policies Tab - History of Activated Policies.

The Paging Mechanism

In all tabs, whenever a list goes beyond the size of the configured row limit, the list is presented in multiple pages. You use the paging options (top right side of the My Active Reports area) to view the next and previous pages of the list.
